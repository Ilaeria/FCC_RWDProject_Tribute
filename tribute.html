Ada Lovelace

The first computer programmer

Here is a timeline of Ada Lovelace's life:

  • 1815 - 10 December: Born Augusta Ada Byron, to Lord and Lady Byron. Ada was Lord Byron's only legitimate child.
  • 1816 - 21 April: Lord Byron orders Lady Byron to leave and take their five-week-old daughter with her.
  • 1824 - 19 April: Lord Byron dies. Ada did not have a significant relationship with her father, and was also not close with her mother.
  • 1828 - Feburary: Ada decides she wants to fly, and begins a research a project to construct wings.
  • 1829 - June: Ada falls severely ill with measles, which left her paralysed and unable to walk unaided for nearly two years.
  • 1832 - Ada's mathematical skill begins to become apparent.
  • 1833 - Ada attempts to elope with a tutor but is caught. The scandal is covered up.
  • 1833 - Ada meets Charles Babbage via a mutual friend, Mary Somerville. She becomes fascinated by his Difference Engine.
  • 1833 - Ada is presented at Court, and quickly becomes popular due to her brilliance.
  • 1835 - 8 July: Ada marries William, 8th Baron King, and becomes Lady King.
  • 1836 - 12 May: Ada and William's first child, Byron, is born.
  • 1837 - 22 September: Ada and William's second child, Anne Isabella (Annabella), is born. Ada suffered an illness for months after the birth of Annabella.
  • 1838 - Due to Ada's connection to the extinct Barons Lovelace, William is made Earl of Lovelace and Viscount Ockham. Ada becomes the Countess of Lovelace.
  • 1839 - 2 July: Ada and William's third child, Ralph, is born.
  • 1843 - William Benjamin Carpenter is assigned by Ada's mother as a tutor for Ada's children. He attempts to start an affair with Ada, who cuts him off.
  • 1843 - Babbage dubs Ada "The Enchantress of Number".
  • 1843 - Ada translates and annotates Luigi Menabrea's article on Babbage's new proposed Analytical Engine. Her notes are published in September 1843. The notes include a method for calculating a sequence of Bernoulli numbers. The method is generally recognised as the world's first computer program, and Ada as the first computer programmer.
  • 1840s - Rumours of affairs circulate around Ada. She courts further scandal due to her love of gambling.
  • 1852 - 27 November: Ada dies from uterine cancer.
